ABU'S SUNDAY OMELETTE

Ingredients:

  • 4 eggs
  • 2 roma tomatoes
  • ½ medium onion
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 10 garlic cloves
  • 1 jalapeño
  • 1 bunch cilantro
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp red chili pepper

Directions:

  1. Cut the tomatoes, onions, and jalapeño into thin slices. Mince the ginger. Chop the cilantro. If you don't have a garlic presser, mince the garlic.
  2. Coat the pan with oil and put it on low heat. Take a garlic presser and squeeze the garlic into the pan. Crank the heat to medium and add the ginger. Stir it around in the oil.
  3. Add the tomatoes next shortly after and stir, cranking the heat to high. Add the salt and red chili pepper and stir.
  4. Wait for the tomatoes to shrink and for the water to evaporate a little.
  5. Change the heat to low and add the onions, then stir. Change the heat to medium and stir for a bit.
  6. Lower the heat, crack all the eggs into the pan and then mix them. While the egg mixture is still wet, add the jalapeños.
  7. Scramble them on medium heat. Abu likes to spray oil on it as he cooks the eggs.
  8. Once the eggs are cooked, add in the cilantro and give it one final stir.
  9. Serve with parata or buttered toast.

ALOO GOSHT

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb goat meat
  • 2 potatoes
  • 1 large yellow onion
  • 2 medium tomatoes
  • 10 cloves garlic
  • 1½ tsp salt
  • 1½ tsp red chili pepper
  • ½ tsp of turmeric
  • 2 tsp cumin
  • 1 inch piece of ginger
  • ½ cup oil
  • 4 cups water

Directions:

  1. Cut fat off goat meat and cut into medium sized pieces. Put goat, onions, oil, garlic, tomatoes, spices (salt, pepper, turmeric, cumin) into pressure cooker.
  2. Pressure cook for five minutes on high heat. If you dont have pressure cooker, leave on medium heat for 30 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, cut and wash potatoes into small pieces.
  4. After five minutes of pressure, open lid and mix. Evaporate water a little bit before adding in the potatoes*.
  5. Add potatoes into mixture and 4 cups of water. Add ginger last.
  6. After potatoes are cooked, put lid on and leave on low heat for five minutes.

*Note: If you make larger serving, take all liquid mixture out of the pressure cooker, leaving only the meat, and place the liquid into another pot to evaporate the water. Keep cooking the meat mixture until you see pools of oil and the mixture is sticking to the meat. Meanwhile cook the potatoes in the liquid mixture in another pot, mixing from time to time. Add ginger after some water has evaporated. Combine both pots and mix.

BHINDI

Ingredients:

  • 4 cup okra
  • 4 onions
  • 4 tomatoes
  • ¾ cup oil
  • 1 tsp red chili pepper
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 lemon or lime

Directions:

  1. Put okra in a pan. Add onions and oil. Place on high heat, stirring occasionally. After 5 to 10 minutes add chopped tomatoes to pan.
  2. Add red chili pepper, salt and turmeric.
  3. Leave for 20 minutes.
  4. It is done when the water evaporates and oil starts to separate.
  5. At the end, you can squeeze a lime or lemon on top.

BIRYANI

Ingredients:

  • 6 onions
  • 6 tomatoes
  • 10 cloves of garlic
  • 1 inch piece of ginger
  • 1 chicken
  • 2 tbsp of salt
  • 2 tbsp ground red chili pepper
  • ½ tbsp turmeric
  • ½ box of Shan Masala Sindi Biryani Spice mix
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 5 to 6 cups basmati rice (or long grain rice)

Optional:

  • 1 bunch cilantro
  • 2 jalapeños
  • 5 dried plums

Directions:

  1. Skin chicken and cut into large pieces (with bones).
  2. Wash rice and leave to soak.
  3. Peel garlic and mince. Peel ginger and mince. Peel onions and chop into medium pieces.
  4. Put all of these into a large cooking pot. Add one cup of oil and cook on high heat, stirring occasionally.
  5. After about 15 minutes, add the pieces of chicken.
  6. Add 3 full spoons of biryani masala
  7. Add 2 spoons salt
  8. Add 2 spoons of red chili pepper
  9. Add a spoon of turmeric
  10. After 10 minutes add diced tomatoes.
  11. After another 10 minutes when the mixture has become less watery, add 1 cup of plain yogurt.
  12. Cook for about 20 minutes more minutes or until mixture is thick, stirring occasionally.
  13. Its done when the mixture is very thick and not watery; make sure the chicken is cooked though.
  14. Cook about 6 cups of rice.
  15. In a pot, put one layer of the biryani masala that you cooked and then add a layer of rice, continue to do this until everything is layered. Mix with spoon about three times. Add cilantro and jalapenos on top.

BUTTER CHICKEN

Ingredients:

  • 3 onions chopped
  • 4 tomatoes chopped
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp red chili pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 2 chicken breasts cubed
  • ½ cup oil

Directions:

  1. Peel and chop 3 onions
  2. Peel and mince 6 pieces of garlic
  3. Put this all in a pot and add ½ cup oil
  4. Cook for 15 minutes
  5. Add 4 chopped tomatoes
  6. Leave covered until all the water from the onions and tomatoes evaporates, mix occasionally
  7. When the mixture is all dry, add small pieces of skinned chicken (no bones)
  8. Add 1 spoon red chili pepper
  9. Add 1 spoon salt
  10. Add ½ spoon turmeric
  11. Cook and stir for 10 minutes
  12. Add 2 cups heavy whipping cream
  13. When it starts boiling, it’s ready.
  14. Taste to see if salt and pepper is fine.
  15. Serve with rice

DAAL

Ingredients:

  • 1½ mug of red split lentils (called Masoor dal in Urdu)
  • 2 mugs of rice
  • 1 tsp red chili pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 1 inch ginger
  • ¼ cup oil

Directions:

  1. Wash the lentils and leave to soak
  2. Leave 4 mugs of water in a pot to boil
  3. When the water starts to boil, add the lentils
  4. Add 1 spoon red chili pepper
  5. Add 1 spoon of salt
  6. Add ½ spoon of turmeric
  7. Add 5 minced pieces of garlic
  8. Add 1 inch of peeled and minced ginger
  9. Leave on high heat for about 30 minutes
  10. Add ¼ cup oil
  11. Leave for 5 to 10 minutes
  12. Taste to see if it cooked properly
  13. Serve with rice

KARAI

Ingredients:

  • Whole chicken or 2 lbs goat cut into medium pieces
  • 2 onions
  • 4 tomatoes
  • 10 cloves garlic
  • 2 inches of ginger
  • 2 tsp red chili pepper
  • 2 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 1 cup oil
  • 15 cloves
  • 15 whole black peppers
  • 1 piece cinnamon
  • 2 black cardamom
  • 1 tsp coriander powder or whole
  • 1 jalapeño
  • 1 bunch cilantro

Directions:

  1. Add chicken/goat, onions, garlic, ginger, and spices into pot.
  2. Cook on high heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. When water evaporates, add tomatoes and oil.
  4. Leave on high heat with cover on pot, stir occasionally.
  5. Leave until water evaporates and you can see pools of oil separating, then its done! If it takes too long for water to evaporate, take the cover off the pot. It should take 45 min to 1 hour to cook.
  6. Served with naan or roti.

MULI PARATA

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 daikon grated
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp red chili pepper
  • 1/2 jalapeño (seedless)
  • 1 tbsp cilantro chopped

Directions:

  1. Squeeze water out of daikon.
  2. Mix spices and cilantro with daikon.
  3. Add water to flour and knead dough for roti.
  4. Add 1/2 tsp salt to 2 cups of flour. Make sure flour mixture is firm, not too watery.
  5. Use a rolling pin to roll out dough into two rotis of equal size.
  6. Add 1/2 tsp butter across roti.
  7. Add daikon mixture on top.
  8. Add the second roti on top.
  9. Press the corners together to seal. Place on stove on high heat. Flip carefully, making sure mixture does not fall out. Leave until roti is cooked.

PAKORAS

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups fresh spinach chopped
  • 6 green onions chopped
  • 2 red potatoes thinly chopped
  • 1 yellow onion thinly chopped
  • 2 small eggplants thinly chopped
  • 2 jalapeños finely chopped
  • 2 tsp red chili pepper
  • 2½ tsp salt
  • 3 cup chick pea powder (basin)
  • 3 tsp coriander seeds meshed
  • 1 tbsp red crushed pepper
  • 1 cup cilantro chopped

Directions:

  1. Mix all ingredients into bowl. Add enough water to form smooth batter.
  2. Heat the oil in a deep heavy-based frying pan until a breadcrumb sizzles and turns brown when dropped into it.
  3. Use spoon to drop mixture into hot oil and fry until golden and crispy on outside.
  4. When the oil is hot, carefully lower tablespoons of the batter mixture in batches into the oil and fry for 5-6 minutes, or until the pakoras are crisp and golden-brown and the vegetables have cooked through.
  5. Serve immediately.

PALAU

Ingredients:

  • 1½ cups rice
  • 1 lb chicken or 1lb goat
  • 1¼ onion
  • ⅓ inch ginger piece
  • 9 cloves garlic
  • 2 black cardamom
  • 6 black pepper cloves
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp coriander
  • 5 cloves
  • 3 whole red pepper
  • 1 tsp cumin

Directions:

  1. Put 4 cups water in pot. Add chicken, ¼ onion, garlic, black cardamom, black pepper, cumin, coriander, cloves. and red pepper in pot. Leave on high heat until water boils, then move to medium heat.
  2. In separate pot, put 1 finely chopped medium onion, 4 cloves garlic, and oil. Cook until brown.
  3. After cooking chicken for about 20 minutes, take chicken out of water pot and add it to onion pot. (Goat needs more time to cook.) Cook for about 5 minutes. Then add water mixture to pot. Add 1 teaspoon salt. Add 1 tsp cumin. Add rice. Add a pinch of crushed ginger. Cook rice until water dries, then leave on low heat for 10 minutes.

SAAG

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs spinach chopped (can be frozen)
  • 1 lb broccoli chopped (can be frozen)
  • 1 lb mustard chopped (can be frozen)
  • 10 cloves garlic
  • 2 inch piece of ginger
  • 1 tsp red chili pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 jalapeño
  • ½ bunch cilantro (optional)
  • ¾ cup oil

Directions:

  1. Add spinach, broccoli, mustard, and jalapeño and 5 cloves garlic in a pot or pressure cooker.
  2. Add 1 cup water.
  3. Cook on medium heat until water evaporates.
  4. Meanwhile, shred rest of the garlic and ginger in chopper.
  5. Place in pan with oil and cook until light brown.
  6. Place spinach, broccoli, and mustard mixture in chopper and then add to oil.
  7. Taste to see if there is enough salt and pepper.

SEEKH KABOB

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium onions
  • 6 jalapeños
  • 1 bunch cilantro
  • 1½ cup garlic
  • 10 Tbs ginger (1½ Tbs per pound)
  • 3 pieces bread (optional)
  • 3 bunches green onion
  • 7 tsp cumin seeds fresh ground
  • 4 tsp red pepper
  • 7 tsp salt
  • 4 Tbs crushed red pepper
  • 2 Tbs garam masala
  • 4 Tbs crushed coriander
  • 4 Tbs lemon juice
  • 7 lbs ground beef
  • 3 eggs

Directions:

  1. Grind onions, jalapeños, cilantro, garlic, ginger and bread in food processor.
  2. Chop green onions.
  3. Add cumin seeds, red pepper, salt, crushed red pepper, garam masala, coriander, and lemon juice.
  4. Mix in ground beef.
  5. Add eggs and knead entire mixture.
  6. Bake at 500 degrees for 20 minutes.

SPANISH CHICKEN POTATOES

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds large Yukon gold potatoes, cut into 1 1/2-inch pieces
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed and coarsely chopped
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 1/2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken thighs (5 to 6 thighs)
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2-3 sprigs fresh dill
  • Freshly ground pepper
  • 4 tbsp roughly chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 lemons (1 juiced, 1 cut into wedges)
  • 2 large or 3 medium red onions, halved and thinly sliced
  • Sour cream

Directions:

  1. Preheat to 500 degrees F.
  2. Put the potatoes, garlic, olive oil, 1 tablespoon water and 1/2 teaspoon salt in a large microwave-safe baking dish and toss to coat. Cover with plastic wrap, pierce the plastic in a few places with a knife and microwave 8 minutes to partially cook.
  3. Meanwhile, pat the chicken dry and transfer to a bowl. Sprinkle with the paprika, 1 teaspoon salt and 1/2 teaspoon pepper. Add 2 tablespoons parsley and dill and the lemon juice; toss to coat. Set aside.
  4. Add the potatoes in an even layer to baking dish. Scatter the onions on top. Roast until the potatoes start to brown, about 12 minutes.
  5. Flip the potatoes and lay the chicken pieces on top, adding any accumulated juices from the bowl; return to the oven and roast until the potatoes are tender and the chicken is cooked through, about 12 more minutes. Remove from the oven and top with the remaining 2 tablespoons parsley. Serve with the lemon wedges and sour cream.

STRAWBERRY SPINACH SALAD

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup distilled white vinegar
  • 1/4 tsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp minced onion
  • 10 ounces fresh spinach - rinsed, dried and torn into bite-size pieces
  • 1 quart strawberries - cleaned, hulled
  • and sliced
  • 1/4 cup almonds, blanched and slivered

Directions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the sesame seeds, poppy seeds, sugar, olive oil, vinegar, paprika, and onion.
  2. Cover, and chill for one hour.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the spinach, strawberries and almonds.
  4. Pour dressing over salad, and toss.
  5. Refrigerate 10 to 15 minutes before serving.

SWEET POTATO CASSEROLE

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups sweet potato, cubed (2 sweet potatoes)
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 4 tbsp butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Topping:

  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 1 cup chopped pecans

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees F. Put sweet potatoes in a medium saucepan with water to cover. Cook over medium high heat until tender; drain and mash.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the sweet potatoes, white sugar, eggs, salt, butter, milk, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth. Transfer to a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  3. In medium bowl, mix the brown sugar and flour. Cut in the butter until the mixture is coarse. Stir in the pecans. Sprinkle the mixture over the sweet potato mixture.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven 30 minutes, or until the topping is lightly brown.
